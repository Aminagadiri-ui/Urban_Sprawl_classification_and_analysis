Key Findings

Scientific conclusions and recommendations

Five Key Scientific Findings
Finding 01
Urban expansion confirmed across all four cities (2000–2025)
All study cities show positive urban growth over the 25-year period. Bangkok and Mexico City record the strongest absolute area increases. Paris shows the smallest relative change, consistent with its mature structure and PLU/Grand Paris planning.
Finding 02
2015 Landsat anomaly was a pipeline artefact, not genuine contraction
Atlanta's 2015 V-shaped dip was traced to winter compositing allowing Dilated Cloud (QA Bit 1) halos into the composite. Two-layer fix — Phase 2 seasonal compositing + Phase 7 anomaly interpolation — fully corrects all reported metrics.
Finding 03
Classification accuracy is climate-zone dependent
Paris achieves the highest Urban F1-scores (0.72–0.82) benefiting from strong spectral contrast. Bangkok and Atlanta show lower F1 (0.45–0.72) due to tropical canopy confusion and low-density suburban spectral signatures.
Finding 04
Land consumption outpaces population growth in most city-periods (LCR > 1)
Atlanta shows the most persistent sprawl (LCR > 1.5 in most periods). Paris shows the most balanced recent performance (LCR approaching 1.0 post-2015), consistent with Grand Paris infill incentives.
Finding 05
Urban morphology diverges systematically across climate zones
Bangkok and Mexico City develop more compactly (lower LSI, higher LPI) than Atlanta (high LSI, high PD), reflecting the difference between state-guided dense-corridor development and market-driven dispersed sprawl.
